We created

An educational animation about the identification of target DNA sequences by Lac repressor and CRISPR-Cas9 complex for Johan Elf’s laboratory in Uppsala University. The animation shows scientifically accurate 3D models of different protein complexes and their interaction with DNA.

Idea

The animation shows two different ways of how proteins search for target sites on the DNA genomic sequence: 1D sliding and 3D diffusion. One variant can be used by transcription factors, and the other, for example, by CRISPR complexes.

All the molecular models used in the educational animation are scientifically accurate and shown in atomic detail. We show both molecular motion and conformational changes that happen in the protein complexes during the interaction with DNA.

The first scientifically accurate virtual-reality animation of the Homo sapiens who lived 30,000 years ago (Sungir people) and are believed to be the ancestors of today’s Northern and Eastern Europeans.
